About Me

I’m a Ph.D. candidate in Astrophysics at the Rochester Institute of Technology with a strong foundation in scientific programming, data analysis, and astrophysical modeling. My research spans stellar kinematics, exoplanet atmospheres, and large-scale X-ray data science. I develop and apply advanced algorithms — including Runge-Kutta integrators, clustering techniques, and image processing pipelines — to study the motion, evolution, and physical properties of stars and planetary systems. With experience in Python, FORTRAN, and C++, I combine a passion for space science with deep technical skills to solve complex scientific problems and communicate discoveries through research publications and presentations.

Research

  • Stellar Associations: Built a 3D traceback algorithm in Python to reconstruct past star trajectories from space-based kinematics. Analyzed >10,000 stars to infer physical properties and evolutionary histories.
  • Exoplanet Atmospheres: Observed stellar spectra with a 2.3m telescope and modeled hydrodynamic escape using space telescope data and Runge-Kutta solvers. Benchmarked against published evaporation models.
  • X-ray Telescope Data Science: Studied >20,000 stars across UV, IR, and optical bands; applied regression, clustering, and statistical modeling to correlate stellar properties with X-ray activity. Manuscript submitted.
